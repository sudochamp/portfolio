Darkroom Enlarger Timer

Bunch of gibberish and funny words to make a point about how cool this project is and was to work on. Yeah this thing works on AC power, can you imagine that? It's gonna be alright, I'll have a fire extinguisher nearby just in case too. No matter, the tests work fine on a breadboard with a 9V battery, but this thing needs to be hooked up to the same connection the enlarger will be connected to. Otherwise, I'd need to throw in a USB-C power port or something. Who wants that anyway? No one. Anyway, this thing does something call which is calculate exposure stops the proper logarithmic way and not the linear way which is most commonly done, by beginners I think at least. (I'm a beginner too, that's why I did this whole thing anyways.)

So, we've got 3 button, from left-to-right, decrease stop by -1 interval, start/stop exposure, increase stop by +1 interval. Then a switch for test mode, or print mode, and then a big knob to set the baseline exposure time. To change the stop interval, click the big knob and set your desired interval. You can do any interval in the nominator/denominator format. The / delimeter is replaced with a decimal point on the device. Can't make a / in the 7-segment display without making a 1, amirite?
